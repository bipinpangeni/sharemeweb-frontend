WebRTC vs Cloud Storage: Which is Safer for Your Files?

Published: Oct 12, 2024 • 5 min read

When you need to send a file over the internet, you usually default to attaching it to an email or uploading it to Google Drive or Dropbox. In this guide, we break down the fundamental differences between traditional Cloud Storage and modern WebRTC Peer-to-Peer (P2P) file sharing.

The Problem with Cloud Storage

Cloud storage operates on a "Client-Server" model. When you upload a file to a service like WeTransfer, the file leaves your computer and is stored on their physical servers. This introduces a major vulnerability: the "Man in the Middle." The company hosting your file has the technical capability to scan, read, or analyze your data.

Enter WebRTC: The P2P Revolution

Web Real-Time Communication (WebRTC) is an open-source project supported by Apple, Google, and Microsoft. It allows web browsers to communicate directly with each other without needing an intermediate server.

  • Zero Storage: Because files flow directly from Browser A to Browser B, there is no central server storing your data.
  • End-to-End Encryption: WebRTC mandates DTLS encryption. It cannot be turned off.
  • Faster Speeds: If you and your recipient are on the same WiFi network, a WebRTC transfer happens locally at maximum router speed.

5 Reasons You Should Stop Emailing Large Attachments

Published: Oct 08, 2024 • 4 min read

We've all been there: you attach a presentation or a video to an email, hit send, and get an error message stating "File exceeds 25MB limit."

1. File Size Limits

Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo all cap attachments at roughly 20 to 25 Megabytes. In an era where a 30-second 4K video shot on an iPhone is over 100MB, email limits are severely outdated.

2. Cluttered Inboxes

When you email a 20MB file to 5 people, you are generating 100MB of data that sits permanently in their inboxes, eating up their storage quotas.

3. Lack of Security

Standard email is notoriously insecure. Emails bounce through multiple servers before reaching their destination, often unencrypted.

The Solution

Instead of relying on outdated email protocols, use a dedicated file-sharing tool like ShareMeWeb. For massive files, use our P2P QR Code scanner. For offline sending, upload the file to our Cloud Vault.

How to Transfer Huge Video Files from iPhone to Windows PC

Published: Oct 02, 2024 • 6 min read

Apple's AirDrop is incredible—if you own a Mac. But what happens if you shoot a stunning 4K video on your iPhone and need to edit it on your Windows PC? You can't use a lightning cable easily due to driver issues, and uploading to iCloud takes hours. Here is the fastest, free method.

Step 1: Open ShareMeWeb on your PC

Open your Chrome or Edge browser on Windows and navigate to ShareMeWeb.com. Click the "Receive File" tab and click "Show My QR Code."

Step 2: Scan with your iPhone

Open the default Camera app on your iPhone and point it at your PC screen. A link will pop up. Tap it to open ShareMeWeb on your phone.

Step 3: Select your Video

On your phone, tap the upload zone and select "Photo Library". Choose your massive video file. Because ShareMeWeb uses your local WiFi network, the video will zip from your phone to your PC's downloads folder at blazing speeds—no cloud upload required!
