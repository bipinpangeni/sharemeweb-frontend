Technical Deep Dive

How to Share Files Online Free — Complete Guide

A plain-English explanation of the technology that makes instant, private file transfers possible.

The Short Version

When you share a file with ShareMeWeb, it travels directly from your browser to the recipient's browser. Our servers are only used for less than a second at the very beginning to help the two browsers "find" each other. After that, we're out of the picture entirely.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

1

Sender selects a file

The file stays in your device's memory — it is not uploaded anywhere at this stage.

2

Receiver scans the QR code

Using their phone camera or a link, the receiver connects directly to your device via WebRTC.

3

File is chunked and streamed

Your browser sends the file in tiny encrypted chunks. The receiver's browser reassembles them locally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a file size limit?

There is no server-imposed size limit on our P2P tool because files never touch our servers. For our Cloud Vault, the limit is 100MB per batch.

Is the transfer encrypted?

Yes. WebRTC mandates DTLS encryption. This is enforced at the browser level — it cannot be disabled.
